Before and After

Original

State estimation is important for distribution networks because operators need accurate voltage states and the measurements are often sparse and noisy, and this creates a difficult optimization problem. In this paper, a neural initialization is proposed and it is combined with weighted least squares. This improves convergence. The method is evaluated on IEEE 33-bus and CIGRE networks, which are standard benchmarks, and the results show lower error and faster runtime compared with flat starts.

Revised

Distribution network operators need accurate voltage states, but field measurements are often sparse and noisy. These measurement limits make state estimation a difficult optimization problem. This paper combines a neural initialization with weighted least squares. This initialization improves convergence. We evaluate the method on IEEE 33-bus and CIGRE benchmark networks, and the results show lower error and faster runtime than flat starts.
